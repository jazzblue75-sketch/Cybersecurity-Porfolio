# TryHackMe: Defensive Security Intro

## Objective
Learn the basics of defensive cybersecurity, how defenders protect systems, and the role of a SOC analyst.

## Defensive Security Explained
Defensive security — also called **Blue Teaming** — focuses on preventing intrusions, detecting attacks, and responding quickly when they occur. It is opposite the offensive (red team) side.

## Key Concepts Learned
- **Blue Team:** cybersecurity professionals who protect an organization’s systems.  
- **Security Operations Center (SOC):** a team that monitors networks to detect suspicious events.  
- **Threat Intelligence:** gathering information about attackers and their techniques.  
- **Digital Forensics and Incident Response (DFIR):**  
  - **Digital Forensics** investigates what happened during an attack.  
  - **Incident Response** follows stages like detection, containment, and recovery.  
- **Malware:** types like ransomware that can harm systems.  
- **SIEM Tools:** simulated in the lab to explore alerts and respond to incidents.

## Skills Practiced
- Understanding defensive security roles  
- Intro to SOC workflows  
- Analyzing simulated alerts

## Why This Matters
Defensive security is critical in real-world jobs like SOC Analyst, Threat Intelligence Analyst, and DFIR roles — all of which involve protecting systems, spotting threats, and responding effectively.
